# Research Recipes

This guide provides checklists and prompts for conducting systematic literature reviews and synthesizing complex research outputs.

---

## 1. Literature Review Checklist

Ensure that the literature research is comprehensive, bias-free, and systematic.

### Research Checklist
- [ ] Keywords and search strings fully documented.
- [ ] Reference databases (IEEE, ACM, Google Scholar, custom) enumerated.
- [ ] Inclusion and exclusion criteria defined for articles.
- [ ] Extraction grid mapped (Author, Year, Key Claim, Methodology, Limitations).
- [ ] Stale or non-peer-reviewed references explicitly flagged.
- [ ] Conflicting results highlighted and discussed.

---

## 2. Research Synthesis Prompt

Synthesize multiple technical articles or docs into a coherent knowledge piece.

### Synthesis Prompt
```markdown
System: You are a technical research architect. Synthesize the findings from the attached source research documents.

Source Documents:
[Insert Source Texts]

Synthesis Goals:
- Group findings into thematic trends.
- Extract concrete evidence and statistics.
- Highlight contradictions or discrepancies between sources.
- List open questions and potential areas of future research.
- Retain all citations and authors.
```

---

## 3. Evaluation of Research Quality

Audit a research summary for methodology validation.

### Research Quality Audit
- [ ] Are claims backed by empirical data or mathematical proofs?
- [ ] Is the sample size or test configuration documented?
- [ ] Are potential conflicts of interest disclosed?
- [ ] Can the experiments be reproduced based on the description?
- [ ] Are variables and control configurations explicitly declared?
